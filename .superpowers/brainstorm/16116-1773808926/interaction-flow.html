Assessment Flow — How Does the Wheel Progress?

The analyst works outward from COM-B hub. How should deficit input work on the wheel itself?

A

Click-to-Cycle Segments

Each COM-B segment starts grey (unassessed). Click cycles through: Adequate (green) → Deficit (amber) → Major Barrier (red) → back to unassessed. Once all 6 are assessed, the intervention ring lights up showing only relevant interventions. Click interventions to select them, then the policy ring activates.

Minimal UI — the wheel IS the form. Fast for experienced users.
B

Click-to-Open Side Panel

Click a COM-B segment to open a slide-out panel with the full assessment form (deficit level, evidence notes, supporting evidence). The segment color updates when you save. Intervention ring appears after all COM-B segments are assessed. Click an intervention segment to see its evidence base, examples, and a "Select" toggle in a panel.

Richer data capture per segment. Familiar panel UX. Wheel stays visible alongside.
C

Guided Sequential Walk-Through

The wheel highlights one COM-B segment at a time, pulsing to guide attention. A panel opens for that segment's assessment. After completing it, the wheel auto-advances to the next segment (with ability to jump). Once all 6 are done, the intervention ring animates in. Same guided approach for selecting interventions, then policies.

Best for onboarding new analysts. Enforces Michie's methodology order. Most "wizard-like."
My recommendation: Option B (Side Panel)

It captures the rich data your existing COM-B forms already support (evidence notes, supporting evidence) while keeping the wheel as the primary visual anchor. Option A is too minimal for real intelligence analysis. Option C is great for training but constraining for experienced analysts who want to jump around.
