Ring Reveal Strategy — How Do Outer Layers Appear?

As the analyst completes COM-B assessment, when and how do intervention & policy rings appear?

A

Progressive Unlock (Strict)

Only the COM-B hub is visible initially. After ALL 6 components are assessed, the intervention ring animates outward (scale + fade). After interventions are selected, the policy ring animates outward. Locked rings show a subtle dashed outline as a teaser.

Enforces methodology Can feel restrictive
B

Progressive Reveal (Soft)

All three rings are visible from the start, but outer rings are ghosted/dimmed (10% opacity). As COM-B deficits are marked, relevant intervention segments start glowing — the ring "wakes up" segment by segment. Same for policies once interventions are selected. Non-relevant segments stay dimmed.

Shows full structure upfront Visual cause-effect
C

Always Visible (Free Explore)

All rings fully visible and interactive from the start. Analysts can work in any order — assess COM-B, browse interventions, check policies freely. Connections between layers are shown via hover highlights (hover a COM-B segment → connected interventions glow). No enforcement of hub→rim order.

Maximum flexibility Doesn't teach the methodology
My recommendation: Option B (Progressive Reveal / Soft)

It communicates the full BCW structure immediately (practitioners expect to see three rings), while the "waking up" animation creates a satisfying cause-and-effect feedback loop: mark a deficit → watch the relevant interventions light up. This is the most educational approach — analysts see the COM-B→intervention mapping happen in real time.
